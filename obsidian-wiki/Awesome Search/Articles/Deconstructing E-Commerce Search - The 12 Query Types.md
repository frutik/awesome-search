# Deconstructing E-Commerce Search: The 12 Query Types

**Source:** https://baymard.com/blog/ecommerce-search-query-types
**Author:** Baymard Institute

## Summary

Taxonomy of the 12 types of queries users enter into e-commerce search engines, based on extensive user testing. Essential reference for [[Query Types]] in the e-commerce domain.

## The 12 Query Types

### Product-Type Queries
1. **Exact product queries** — searching for a specific model ("iPhone 15 Pro Max")
2. **Category queries** — broad product categories ("headphones")
3. **Feature/spec queries** — searches by attribute ("wireless noise cancelling headphones")
4. **Thematic queries** — searches by theme/style ("minimalist desk accessories")

### Non-Product Queries
5. **Symptom queries** — describing a problem, not a product ("my back hurts pillow")
6. **Compatible product queries** — accessories for a known item ("case for MacBook Air M2")
7. **Use-case queries** — activity-based ("running shoes for marathons")
8. **Named product queries** — product names with unknown classification

### Navigation Queries
9. **Brand queries** — product + brand ("Nike shoes")
10. **Price-range queries** — budget-constrained ("desk under $200")
11. **Availability queries** — store/format ("books available for pickup")
12. **Inspirational queries** — exploratory browsing ("gift ideas for dad")

## Key Finding

Most e-commerce sites optimize for category queries but fail on non-product, symptom, and inspirational queries — which represent significant user intent that current systems can't match to inventory.
