# Search-Log Analysis: The Most Overlooked Opportunity in Web UX Research

**Source:** https://www.nngroup.com/articles/search-log-analysis/ — [[Nielsen Norman Group]]

Site-search logs are a direct, underused record of what visitors actually want and where they fail — "one of the biggest missed opportunities in UX research." This is a UX-research framing of [[Search Observability]].

## What Logs Reveal (seven insight categories)

1. **Unmet needs** — content gaps and navigation failures
2. **Non-existent content** — demand for topics the site doesn't cover
3. **Vocabulary mismatch** — user terms vs. site language (drives [[Synonyms]])
4. **Misspellings / poor results** — search-quality issues ([[Spelling Correction]])
5. **Blank searches** — IA workarounds or technical problems
6. **Extended queries** — user distress or abuse
7. **Numerical searches** — missing product-lookup capability

## Techniques

- Top ~1,000 query terms by frequency over 3–6 months
- Reconstruct sessions by identifier + timestamp
- Pull queries returning [[Zero Results|zero results]] and the longest queries
- Cluster queries by meaning (synonym discovery)
- Feed findings into IA, navigation, content, localization, "best bets," and engine tuning
- Strip PII before sharing
