# Good Abandonment on Search Results Pages

**Source:** https://www.nngroup.com/articles/good-abandonment/ — [[Nielsen Norman Group]]

**Good abandonment** is when the user's need is fully satisfied by the results page itself — no click and no reformulation needed. As engines become answer engines (featured snippets, knowledge panels), a no-click session is often a success, not a failure.

## Why It Matters for Metrics

- Treating all abandonment as failure misreads modern SERPs. ~35% of desktop and ~62% of mobile searches end without a click.
- This complicates [[Click Signals|click-based]] success metrics: a zero-click query can be a win (answer shown) or a loss (nothing relevant). Raw CTR can't tell them apart.
- Argues for session- and intent-aware evaluation over click-only proxies — see [[Session-Based Evaluation]].

## The Risk Side

Good abandonment goes bad when extracted snippets lack context or are wrong: users ("satisficers" exhibiting "Google gullibility") trust an incomplete answer and leave with the wrong information.
