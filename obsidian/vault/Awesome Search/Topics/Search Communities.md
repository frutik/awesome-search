# Search Communities

The **people networks** of the search and information-retrieval world: identity-based communities, meetups, Slack groups, and open-source communities where practitioners connect, mentor, share knowledge, and hire. Distinct from [[Events and Conferences]] (the *events* where these communities gather) — this note is about the *standing communities* themselves.

---

## Identity & Mentorship Communities

### [[Women of Search]]
International community empowering and celebrating women in search. Founded May 2021 by **[[Audrey Lorberfeld]]**. Runs a mentorship program, a blog/resources, and presents technical talks at conferences (notably Haystack US/EU).

🔗 https://www.women-of-search.org/

## Meetups

### London Search & AI Meetup
In-person meetup organized by **[[Charlie Hull]]** / [[The Search Juggler]]. A core gathering point for the European search community.

## Slack & Online Communities

### Relevance Slack
OpenSource Connections-run Slack community for search relevance practitioners — one of the most active online hubs for the field.

### OpenSource Connections Community
The community around OpenSource Connections (OSC), closely tied to the open-source search ecosystem and the Haystack conference.

---

## Why Communities Matter

Search is a small, specialized field. Communities are where practitioners:

- **Learn** — talks, mentorship, and shared write-ups compress years of tacit knowledge.
- **Get hired** — much of [[Hiring for Search]] flows through community pipelines, not job boards.
- **Break in** — for newcomers, communities are the most welcoming on-ramp (see [[How to Start a Career in Search]]).
- **Stay current** — Slack groups and meetups surface emerging practice ahead of formal publication.
