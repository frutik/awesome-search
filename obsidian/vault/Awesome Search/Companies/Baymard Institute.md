# Baymard Institute

Independent UX research institute focused on **e-commerce usability**. Baymard runs large-scale, qualitative usability studies and maintains a benchmark database of e-commerce sites, turning observed user behavior into concrete, prioritized design guidelines. Much of its published research targets **on-site search and discovery** — query types, autocomplete, faceted navigation, search scopes, no-results pages, and search-results presentation.

Its house style is the quantified guideline — "*N% of sites get this wrong*" — derived from testing real shoppers against real interfaces rather than opinion or vendor benchmarks. This makes Baymard one of the most-cited empirical sources for [[Search UX]] decisions.
