# Nielsen Norman Group

UX research and consulting firm (NN/g), founded by Jakob Nielsen and Don Norman. NN/g produces evidence-based usability research across the web, much of it directly relevant to **search interfaces**: how users scan search-results pages, when scoped search helps or harms, what makes a good no-results page, and how to mine search logs for UX insight.

Where [[Baymard Institute]] concentrates on e-commerce, NN/g's search research is broader (content sites, enterprise, web at large), making the two complementary empirical sources for [[Search UX]].

## Notable Search-UX Findings

- **Pinball pattern** — complex results pages push users into non-linear scanning
- **Love-at-first-sight gaze pattern** — how users fixate on the first compelling result
- **Scoped search** — useful but risky; stale scopes mislead users (see [[Search Scopes]])
- **No-results SERPs** — guidelines for recovering a failed query (see [[Zero Results]])
- **Search-log analysis** — using query logs as a UX research method ([[Search Observability]])
- **Good abandonment** — not every query that ends without a click is a failure
